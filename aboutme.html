Bio

As a catalyst for innovation and entrepreneurship in social impact, I find purpose in empowering individuals, institutions, and communities through collaboration across traditional boundaries of disciplines and borders. I am currently an MBA candidate at Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management where I am deepening my financial, strategic, and analytical acumen to be a more effective social impact leader.

With extensive experience in the nonprofit sector and global health space, I enjoy tackling big problems in an interdisciplinary manner, driving evidence-based solutions, and navigating the ambiguity that comes with these challenges. Skilled in development and management of organizations, projects, relationships, and processes, my journey has led me to:

  • Co-found and grow an international development nonprofit with a $5.3M grant portfolio across 12 countries in the Middle East & North Africa, Asia, and Sub-Saharan Africa and a team in Washington, DC and Lahore, Pakistan
  • Design and implement programming for young entrepreneurial leaders in science around the world, including Pakistan, Iraq, Indonesia, India, Nigeria, and others
  • Launch a new science magazine with readership of 40,000+ individuals in 150+ countries
  • Advise social enterprises in Ghana and Zambia on U.S. market penetration, digital optimization, and business development
  • Collaborate with a community health in Ecuador to develop a peer health education curriculum and microfinance program for at-risk adolescents
  • Conduct both laboratory and public policy analytical research on global health challenges with high disease burden such as tuberculosis and non-communicable diseases
